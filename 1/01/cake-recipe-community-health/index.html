Cake Recipe Community Health

January 1, 1

This is an analysis of the volume and authorship of cake recipes published on Food52’s community dirven recipe database. Covered in this post are general trends on the amount of recipes published, who’s publishing the reicpes and what patterns exist in the communtiy. It was discovered that while the rate of new authorship and the activity of frequent authors has declined since 2014, the amount published per author has increased to keep the total number of new recipes added stable.

Cumulative number of recipes published over time

Over time, the annual average rate of cake recipes added per day has ranged between 1 recipe every 2 days (.5 per day) and 6 recipes every 5 days (1.2 recipes per day). 2014 was the period of highest growth in the collection of recipes with an average of 1.2 recipes per day. The plot below highlights this period in yellow.

The plot below also shows that currently the number of cake recipes added per day is cooling down slowly from its high in 2014. The year to date average number of recipes added per day is just over 1 per day. This average rate of increase still allows for great diversity and breadth of new cakes to be added to the community collection of recipes, though further slowing of the rate may indicate the community is less engaged with contributing new material.

Distinct Authors over time and their contributions

During late 2013-2014, the number of distinct recipe authors per month increased to their highest levels. Since this period, the number of distinct authors has slowly declined to an average of 19 authors per month in 2017.

Interestingly, this decrease in the number of distinct authors has been compensated by the rapid growth in contributions per author. In the plot below the increase in contributrions per author is noted by color, more red indicates higher average contributions per author. In 2017, the average monthly contributions per author is 7, this is a 75% increase in authorship since 2015-2016 and a 130% increase from 2013-2014. This trend is driven primarily by professional chefs (‘Oh Sweet Day!’, ‘Jordan Rondel, The Caker’,Alice Medrich) and Food52 staff (Sarah Jampel, Food52).

More red lines indicate higher rates of recipes contributed per recipe author

New Authors

The rate of new authors contributing to Food52 has increased so far in 2017 relative to the slow down in new author contributions frm 2015-2016. Combining this plot with the previous plots show new authors were the primary contributors to the recipe boom of 2014.

Single/Multiple Contributors

As the data has shown so far, new authors are very important to increasing the number of recipes added to the community cake collection. The data has also shown that multiple contributions from a single author can have a large impact. The difference in contributions are shown below. Those that have contributed more than one recipe are classifed as as ‘multiple contributors’ and those that have contributed one recipe as ‘single contributors’. The plot shows in recent years there is a widening gap in contributions between the two groups. This is in part due to a decreased rate of new authorship, but there appears to be more than a simple relationship in the data.

Model the differences

A model based approach lets us see the more nuanced differences between the two groups of contributors. Many possible variables were tested to analyze the difference in the groups, but the most explanatory variables are time and the presence of an active contest.

On average, both single and multiple contributor groups are expected to contribute more recipes over time, but at different rates. The model projects recipe contributions to increase by one every 4 months for multiple contributors and one every 14 months for single contributors.

Active contests were also found to increase the expected amount of new recipes added by both groups. The impact of contests were found by investigating the spikes found at semi regular points on the above graph. An example can be seen in November of 2014 with the ‘Your best Breakfast Baked Good’ contest. Both groups saw a spike in contributions above what would normally be expected.
Active contests were found to be dependent on the type of contributor. The monthly expected recipe count increases by 16 for multiple contributors and by 10 for single contributors

Active Multiple contributors

As shown above, on average multiple contributors add more recipes per month than single contributors and they are expected to continue to do so. By summing the total number of recipes contributed by group, multiple contributors have authored 67% of the cake recipes in this data while only comprising 31% of the population of authors.

With this in mind, lets look at the survivorship of multiple contributors.

For this analysis, an ‘active conttributor’ is a multiple contributor who has published in the last 135 days. 135 days was chosen because it is the 75% percentile of days between publishing for multiple contributors. This means that 75% of the intra publishing times for new recipes is less than 135 days.

By totalling the number of active contributors over every day within the data, we get the plot below. The plot below shows a decrease in the number of active multiple contributors in recent history.

Summary

The number of people contributing to the community based collection of cake recipes on Food52 is not as large as it has been, but because of the increased rate of per author publications the realative number of new recipes the commuity enjoys each month has remained high. To ensure the continued vigor in the community, Food52 should continue to do all it can to increase enagement of multiple contributors and new contributors alike. Well publicized competitions and growing the vibrant community of users are proven steps to do this.
